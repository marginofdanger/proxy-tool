9. Key Patterns & Observations

  • CIC provisions are standard: 72 of 181 companies with severance data include change-in-control provisions. The median CEO CIC package is $19.4M, reflecting the norm of 2-3x base salary plus equity acceleration.
  • The golden parachute premium is significant: CEOs receive a median of 28.8% more in a CIC termination than a regular termination without cause. 4 companies have CIC premiums exceeding 200% of base severance, driven primarily by accelerated equity vesting.
  • CEO transitions reset severance: 33 year-over-year changes involved a CEO transition. New CEOs often negotiate different severance terms — sometimes higher (for external hires who demand protection), sometimes lower (for internal promotions with existing equity stakes).
  • Provisions are being added, not removed: 10 instances of companies adding severance provisions vs only 8 removals. The trend is toward more comprehensive protection, not less.
  • Year-over-year changes are common: 41 packages increased >25% and 30 decreased >25% between consecutive filings. Severance is not a "set it and forget it" provision — it's renegotiated regularly as CEO tenure, equity positions, and board dynamics evolve.
  • Equity acceleration dominates CIC packages: For companies with component breakdown data, equity acceleration is typically the largest component, often exceeding cash severance. This means the real "cost" of a golden parachute depends heavily on the executive's unvested equity position and the acquirer's stock price.

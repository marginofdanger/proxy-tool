2. Methodology

Grant Date Approximation

Mega-grants are identified from SEC DEF 14A filings' Summary Compensation Tables (SCT). The SCT reports compensation by fiscal year, not the exact grant date. We approximate the grant date as January 2 of the fiscal year (the first typical trading day). This means actual grant dates could differ, affecting calculated returns.

Benchmark

Each grant is benchmarked against the S&P 500 (^GSPC) over the identical time period. Alpha = Grant CAGR minus S&P 500 CAGR over the same period.

Formulas

  • Total Return = (Current Price / Grant-Date Price - 1) × 100
  • CAGR = (Current Price / Grant-Date Price)^(1/years) - 1 × 100
  • Alpha = Stock CAGR - S&P 500 CAGR (same period)
  • Max Drawdown = Largest peak-to-trough decline since grant date
  • Annualized Volatility = Std(daily log returns) × √252 × 100
  • Information Ratio = (CAGR - S&P CAGR) / (Tracking Error × 100)
  • Est. Current Value = (Grant Value / Grant Price) × Current Price

Limitations

  • Grant date is approximated as Jan 2 of the fiscal year, not the actual board approval date.
  • Stock awards vs option awards are lumped together; options may have different strike prices.
  • Stock splits, dividends, and other corporate actions are handled by yfinance's adjusted close.
  • Grants from recent fiscal years (< 1 year) are flagged and use total return instead of CAGR.
